Link2Care App


Link2Care connects carers with like-minded people in similar positions, to get advice and support, as well as linking them with industry experts for 24/7 access to professional advice.


The Team
Kate Hawkin, Mana Asif, Jess Southey, Olga Peshkova

Project Overview

Caregiving is a broad industry with many levels of expertise and requirements. For non-professional carers it can be very demanding and isolating when they don’t have the support they need.

Link2Care provides them with the opportunity to form ongoing relationships with people who understand what they’re going through. It aims to be there for the carer, so that they can feel confident and be the best caregiver possible for their loved ones.

Design Process

Research

Objectives

The impact this line of work has on care givers
Type of support or resources they need
Understand if and/or how carers look after their own health

Interviews

2 x proffessional aged carers
2 x non-proffessional carers

Survey

Received more than 15 responces from professional and non-professional carers

Key Findings

1 Non-professionals carers are largely unpaid and often working full-time or part-time to support themselves and their families.

2 Family carers have very little to no training as a caregiver and are often thrown into challenging situations that they are not prepared for.

3 They are often the main point of contact for their loved ones emotional well-being as well as taking care of their basic needs which can be very confronting as a family member.

4 These carers are spending all of their spare time in their caregiving role which makes outside commitments much harder to manage.

5 When in need of support, they often don’t know where to look, unless they personally know someone in a similar situation.

6 Most have said they don’t reach out for help and do not prioritize getting support for themselves, even though they are often overburdened, stressed and overwhelmed.

User Persona

The Problem


Non-professional caregivers who care for the elderly, disabled or otherwise dependant family member are often isolated and overwhelmed in their roles due to the inherent lack of support structure. These caregivers need more access to tools to help them find support, get advice and share resources.

Feature Prioritisation

User Flow


Our user flow covers 3 main objectives;
Onboarding process where the user has the choice of registering with Link2Care, or sign in using their Facebook or Google account
Option to send a direct message to an expert in a specialised field
And the ability to browse the community forum, search for specific topics, and finally make a new post if a suitable answer hadn’t been found

Wireframe Sketching


In the early stages of design, we focused on developing 'Community Forums' and 'Ask an Expert' features, with reference to self-care and scheduling options.

User Testing
& Iterations


Our testing phase has revealede a number of issues, leading to multiple rounds of iterations. Phrasing and terminology made clearer
Buttons are now more descriptive, to avoid confusion
Functionality changes, such as showing when experts are online and available
Introduced a drop down menus for category selections, as well as filtering options for easy discovery
